**EPA vs DHA:**
| Feature | EPA | DHA |
|---|---|---|
| Primary role | Anti-inflammatory, anti-clotting | Cell membrane integrity, brain structure |
| Platelet effect | Reduces stickiness | Less studied |
| Heart rate | Improves HRV | Beneficial rhythm effects |
| Depression | More effective | Less evidence |
| Triglycerides | Strong reducer | Also effective |
| HDL | Less effect | Increases HDL2 particles |

- Average American (no supplementation): ~150 mg EPA+DHA/day

- **Take with food** for better absorption

- Recommended brands: **Carlson, Nordic Naturals**

### REDUCE-IT Trial — Attia's Critical Analysis

**Trial:** 8,179 statin-treated patients with CVD or diabetes; 4.9-year follow-up  
**Intervention:** Icosapent ethyl (purified EPA, 4g/day) — brand name Vascepa  
**Result:** 17.2% MACE vs 22.0% in controls — 5% absolute risk reduction → FDA approved

**The Placebo Problem (Ridker et al. secondary analysis):**
The mineral oil placebo was not inert. The control group showed:
- IL-6: +26%
- IL-1β: +48%
- Homocysteine: +9.5%
- Lipoprotein(a): +8%

The icosapent ethyl group showed **no change** in these markers — suggesting apparent benefit may partly reflect placebo harm.

**Comparison:** STRENGTH trial (corn oil placebo) showed no such biomarker deterioration, supporting the mineral oil contamination hypothesis.

**Attia's Conclusion:** Without a truly neutral placebo and without a clear mechanism explaining how pure EPA reduces CV risk, the FDA should reconsider recommendations pending further investigation.

## 2. Examine.com — Evidence Summary

*(Source: examine.com/supplements/fish-oil/ and examine.com/supplements/omega-3-fatty-acids/ — site returns 403; data from search summaries)*

### Health Outcomes by Category

**Cardiovascular:**
- Reduces triglycerides — consistent, well-established
- No clear effect on rate of cardiovascular events in most trials
- Improves flow-mediated dilation of brachial artery (meta-analysis of RCTs)
- High-dose supplementation associated with **increased atrial fibrillation risk** (see below)

**Atrial Fibrillation Risk (major concern):**
- Meta-analysis of 8 RCTs (83,112 participants): omega-3 supplementation → **+24% relative risk of AF**
- Dose-dependent:
  - ~1g/day: **+12% AF risk**
  - 1.8–4g/day: **~+50% AF risk**
- Dietary intake at moderate levels (~650 mg/day DHA+EPA) appears **protective** (12% AF risk reduction)
- Mechanism: dose-dependent increase in vagal tone — low-level vagal stimulation is protective; high-level is arrhythmogenic

**Brain / Cognitive:**
- Promotes blood flow to brain in healthy adults with **low dietary fish intake**
- Does **not** improve cognition in healthy adults
- Small benefit for adults with **low episodic memory at baseline**
- 2.3g/day slows cognitive decline in Alzheimer's patients
- 2g/day DHA improves cognition in mild cognitive impairment

**Depression:**
- **1g EPA/day** appears most effective for depression
- EPA > DHA for mood effects

**Inflammation:**
- Anti-inflammatory effects, especially EPA
- Reduces pro-inflammatory eicosanoids derived from arachidonic acid

### Forms
- **Triglyceride (TG) form:** Better absorbed (~70% more than ethyl ester form)
- **Ethyl ester (EE) form:** Most common, cheaper, still effective but lower bioavailability
- **rTG (re-esterified triglyceride):** Premium, best absorption profile

## 4. IFOS Certification — Nutrasource

**Program:** International Fish Oil Standards (IFOS™)  
**Operator:** SGS Nutrasource  
**Founded:** 2004  
**Scale:** 200+ certified brands, 15,000+ product SKUs tested

### What IFOS Tests

| Parameter | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Omega-3 content** | Actual EPA+DHA vs. label claim |
| **Peroxide Value (PV)** | Primary oxidation (fresh rancidity) |
| **Anisidine Value (AV)** | Secondary oxidation (aldehydes, longer-term rancidity) |
| **TOTOX** | Total oxidation: `2 × PV + AV` |
| **Contaminants** | PCBs, dioxins, furans, heavy metals (mercury, lead, cadmium, arsenic) |
| **Stability** | Shelf-life and degradation resistance |

### 5-Star IFOS Thresholds

| Metric | 5-Star Standard |
|---|---|
| Peroxide Value (PV) | ≤ 5 meq/kg |
| Anisidine Value (AV) | ≤ 20 |
| TOTOX | ≤ 26 |

**TOTOX formula:** `TOTOX = (2 × PV) + AV`  
GOED (Global Organization for EPA and DHA Omega-3) calls TOTOX "the most important indicator to determine the quality of fish oil."

### Why Oxidation Matters

Rancid fish oil is not just unpleasant — oxidized EPA and DHA can be converted into **pro-inflammatory free radicals**, potentially reversing the supplement's intended benefits.

### Key Differentiator

IFOS tests by **individual lot number** — consumers can verify the specific batch they purchased, not just a general brand certification.

**Certified product search:** https://certifications.nutrasource.ca/certified-products  
*(Filter by "Omega Fatty Acids" category; search by brand or lot number)*

## 5. Clinical Evidence Summary (2025 PMC Review)

**Source:** *N-3 Fatty Acids (EPA and DHA) and Cardiovascular Health — Updated Review* (PMC12628397, 2025)

### Mechanisms

- **Lipid metabolism:** Reduces triglycerides via enhanced hepatic fatty acid oxidation + decreased *de novo* lipogenesis
- **Anti-inflammatory:** Modulates eicosanoid production, reduces inflammatory cytokines
- **Antiarrhythmic:** Ion channel regulation via PIEZO1 protein effects (at low doses)
- **Endothelial function:** Improves flow-mediated dilation
- **Gut-heart axis:** Modulates gut microbiome composition

### Major RCTs

| Trial | Dose | Formulation | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **REDUCE-IT** (2019) | 4g/day | Pure EPA (icosapent ethyl) | −25% MACE; controversial due to mineral oil placebo |
| **STRENGTH** (2020) | 3g/day (2.2g EPA + 0.8g DHA) | EPA+DHA | No CV benefit; terminated early |
| **VITAL** (2019) | 840mg/day | EPA+DHA | No primary outcome benefit; −28% MI |
| **ASCEND** (2018) | 840mg/day | EPA+DHA | No effect on major vascular events in diabetics |

### Key Interpretation

- **EPA monotherapy** performed better than combined EPA+DHA in RCTs
- **Moderate intake (~650–750 mg/day)** appears optimal for cardiovascular protection
- **Higher doses** show diminishing returns or increased AF risk
- Cohort/observational studies consistently show benefit; RCT results are heterogeneous

### Dose-Response for Atrial Fibrillation Risk

| Dose | AF Risk Change |
|---|---|
| ~650 mg/day dietary | −12% (protective) |
| ~1g/day supplement | +12% |
| 1.8–4g/day supplement | ~+50% |

## 6. Practical Synthesis

### Who benefits most from omega-3 supplementation?

- People with **low dietary fish intake** (< 2 servings/week)
- People with **elevated triglycerides**
- People with **depression** (especially EPA 1g/day)
- People with **low omega-3 index** (< 8% on OmegaQuant test)
- Possibly: early cognitive impairment or low baseline episodic memory

### Who should be cautious?

- Anyone with **history of atrial fibrillation** or high AF risk
- People considering **high doses (≥ 2g/day)** — weigh CV benefit vs AF risk
- People taking **blood thinners** (anticoagulant interaction risk)

### Supplement Selection Checklist

1. **IFOS 5-star certified** — verify by lot number at certifications.nutrasource.ca
2. **TOTOX ≤ 26**, PV ≤ 5, AV ≤ 20
3. **Form:** Triglyceride (TG) or re-esterified TG preferred over ethyl ester for absorption
4. **EPA+DHA content:** Verify label — capsules should contain ≥ 80% omega-3
5. **Take with a fatty meal** for optimal absorption
6. **Test baseline** with OmegaQuant before and after (~4 months) to confirm response

### Dosing Guidance

| Goal | Suggested Dose |
|---|---|
| General cardiovascular health | 1–2g EPA+DHA/day |
| Triglyceride reduction | 2–4g EPA+DHA/day (physician supervision) |
| Depression support | 1g EPA/day |
| Omega-3 index optimization | Titrate to OmegaQuant result; target 8–12% |
| High-dose therapeutic (Rx) | Only as icosapent ethyl (Vascepa) with physician guidance |

### Common Themes Across n=1 Reports

**What people most often notice (if anything):**
1. **Mood / emotional stability** — most commonly reported and earliest felt (2–6 weeks), especially with high-EPA products
2. **Joint comfort** — particularly noted by athletes and those with inflammatory conditions
3. **Skin quality** — brighter, less dry, reduced inflammation; often a "surprise" benefit
4. **Dry eye relief** — high-EPA fish oil anecdotally effective; RCT evidence mixed
5. **Triglyceride lab results** — not felt subjectively but confirmed on bloodwork at ~3-month mark

**What people rarely notice directly:**
- Cardiovascular protection (operates silently over years)
- Cognitive changes (in healthy adults without deficiency, changes are subtle)
- OmegaQuant index improvement (requires testing, not felt)

**Timeline expectations (n=1 consensus):**
| Timeframe | What to expect |
|---|---|
| 2–4 weeks | Possible early mood/joint changes (not universal) |
| 4–8 weeks | Skin, dry eye changes if responding |
| 3–4 months | OmegaQuant index reaches new steady-state; retest here |
| 6–12 months | Triglyceride panel changes visible on bloodwork |

### Key n=1 Self-Experiment Framework

If you want to run your own structured experiment:

1. **Baseline test:** OmegaQuant Omega-3 Index before starting (~$50 mail-in test)
2. **Choose an IFOS 5-star certified product** (TG or rTG form, TOTOX ≤ 26)
3. **Start dose:** 2g EPA+DHA/day with a fatty meal; increase to 3–4g if targeting triglyceride reduction (consult physician)
4. **Track subjective markers weekly:** mood, joint comfort, sleep quality, skin, energy
5. **Retest at 4 months:** OmegaQuant index + fasting lipid panel (triglycerides, HDL)
6. **Adjust:** If index < 8%, increase dose or switch to TG-form; if index > 12%, reduce
7. **Flag for physician if:** history of atrial fibrillation, taking anticoagulants, or dose exceeds 3g/day
