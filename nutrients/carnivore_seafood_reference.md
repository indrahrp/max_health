# Carnivore Diet + Seafood: In-Depth Reference

**Compiled:** 2026-05-12  
**Sources:** Local transcripts in `/max_health/carn/` — Anthony Chaffee MD (`anthonychaffeemd`), Shawn Baker MD (`shawnbakermd`), ZeroCarb community (`zerocarb`), Metabolic Mind (`metabolicmind_transcripts.txt`)

---

## Overview

Seafood — fish, shellfish, and other marine animals — sits in an interesting position within the carnivore community. It is universally accepted as animal food and therefore "technically" carnivore, yet debates persist about whether it belongs alongside beef as a primary food or is treated as a peripheral add-on. The transcripts show a spectrum from **pure beef + water (lion diet)** on one end to **flexible animal-based eating that freely includes seafood** on the other.

## 1. Shawn Baker MD (`shawnbakermd`)

**Position:** Fish is his **4th most common food** on carnivore, after beef, eggs, and dairy.

### His Personal Protocol

> *"After dairy, then fish would be my fourth most common. And more often than not, I just — because I'm relatively minimalist and fairly lazy when it comes to cooking, to be honest — I'll often eat things like smoked salmon 'cuz I can just open it up and eat it directly out of the packaging. Try to get wild-caught when I can... a little bit of steak every day, some cottage cheese and salmon."*
> — Video: wHI1XkH3E7k (Top 5 Foods on Carnivore Diet)

> *"I have a lot of things like salmon and shrimp as a sort of sometimes as a side to my meals."*

### The "Fish as Vegetable" Debate

Baker directly addresses the cultural meme (Ron Swanson / Parks & Rec) that dismisses fish:

> *"Fish for sport only, not for meat. Fish meat is practically a vegetable."*  
> — quoting Ron Swanson, then noting he personally does not hold this view

Baker's actual position: he personally consumes fish, does not consider it inferior, but acknowledges the debate exists in the carnivore community.

### Shellfish for Testosterone & Sexual Health

> *"Shellfish is another food — now shellfish has a lot of zinc and we know that zinc has a significant relationship with testosterone and sexual function... things like crab, lobster, oysters — great sources of those."*

> *"Fatty fish like salmon has a number of very important compounds from the omega-3 fats EPA, DHA, proteins, selenium, vitamin D — all these things are important."*
> — Video: 6ZkGXWBDUjY (Testosterone & Red Meat)

### Refrigerator Tour (Video: CgGIeRMRBBM)

> *"For the sake of variety, I'm expanding my normal palette a little just for the sense of displaying what potentially is possible on a carnivore diet... For a little snack, I'm going to have some of this salmon. This is a smoked salmon, just a wild-caught Alaskan salmon... I'll probably eat about half that. That's probably about 4 ounces of salmon."*
> *"Got some wild-caught salmon also again from Costco."*

## 2. Anthony Chaffee MD (`anthonychaffeemd`)

**Position:** Personally prefers beef and feels best on it; does not push fish but acknowledges it as valid. Guests on his show have more diverse seafood-inclusive protocols.

### Chaffee's Own View

> *"I'm in the position that I can sort of eat whatever meat I want, but I always feel best eating just beef and that sort of thing. But there are others that have to be more careful with it and so there are different sorts of things that complement each other — there's different meats and organs and seafood."*
> — Video: bCtJCOOEuv8

### Guest: Surf-and-Turf n=1 Story (Video: bCtJCOOEuv8)

A guest describes a personal evolution:

> *"Before he passed, I was eating a lot of red meat. And then after he passed and after things sort of shifted for me, I started eating more fish. And now I do like a surf-and-turf kind of thing."*

The same guest added shellfish specifically for a micronutrient reason:

> *"I'm sorry — the iron from the meat. And so that's why I added in shellfish because copper helps retain iron and ferritin and that was very important to me. And ever since I made those changes really it's been smooth sailing from there."*

**Key n=1 insight:** Shellfish (particularly oysters, liver-rich in copper) added not for taste but to fix an iron retention problem — copper-zinc-iron interaction as a practical self-experiment.

### Chaffee Guest: Sardine Fasts (Video: GC9SVt5fNn8)

A guest describes extended single-food experiments:

> *"I've done a lot of sardine-related. So I've done like 7, 10, 14-day sardine fasts where that's all I ate. I did before and after blood work and DEXA scans just to see what would happen."*

> *"Very interesting visceral fat loss on a sardine fast. It's basically a protein sparing modified fast."*

**n=1 finding:** 7–14 days on sardines only → measurable visceral fat loss on DEXA; bloodwork tracked before/after.

### Iodine: The Overlooked Seafood Argument (Video: F6r7xoJCSrc)

Chaffee raises a nutritional gap when people eat no seafood:

> *"Most soils are actually pretty deficient in iodine as well, especially in Australia. 100% of the people that I check — check their iodine in Australia on a standard diet unless they eat a lot of seafood — they're low. A lot of fish can sort that out, but a lot of people aren't doing that."*

**Practical implication:** If doing a land-animal-only carnivore diet, iodine becomes a real gap. Seafood is one of the only dietary iodine sources.

### Omega-3s When Eating Grain-Finished Beef (Video: F6r7xoJCSrc)

> *"Adding in some fish, things like that — you have to think about those omega-3s if you're predominantly eating grain-finished."*

Chaffee's logic: grass-fed/finished beef has reasonable omega-3:omega-6 ratios; grain-finished shifts toward omega-6 dominance, making fish a useful corrective.

### Transitioning Ex-Vegetarians via Fish (Video: qd2WDow8ltM)

A guest describes using fish as an on-ramp:

> *"Half of them decided to start eating eggs and fish and chicken etc. So you know, you're not always going to get everyone full carnivore but you're moving them in the right direction."*

And:

> *"I started eating maybe fish and eggs... people are — when they get exposed to the real information — ready to change."*

### Histamine Note for Sensitive Individuals (Chaffee transcripts)

> *"It could be that you need to go low-histamine carnivore... it's like you said, how fresh is it? Is it aged? Is it smoked? You can't — if you have histamine issues — you can't be eating a bunch of smoked brisket... even fish. I do occasionally eat [fish] but..."*

**Caution flag:** Smoked, canned, or aged fish is **high histamine**. People with histamine sensitivity doing carnivore may react to salmon jerky, smoked salmon, sardines in cans — fresh fish is the lower-histamine option.

## 3. ZeroCarb Community (`zerocarb`)

The ZeroCarb channel has **1,092 fish/seafood mentions** — the highest volume of any source. It features community members and practitioners with wide-ranging approaches.

### Definition of Carnivore That Includes Seafood

> *"Just saying carnivore instead of, you know, only eating meats and eggs and possibly seafood and fish — that was different. But she wasn't opposed to it."*
> — Video: PceuXaqVjq0

> *"You can make carnivore pizza. You can eat chicken or pork or seafood if you'd like."*

### Community n=1: 70/30 Diet Split (zerocarb transcripts)

> *"My diet consists of fatty beef with the other 30% consisting of chicken, pork, lamb, tripe, eggs, cheese, fish, and shrimp. All of which I buy at conventional grocery stores. I generally eat twice a day and only drink water, unflavored salts, and bone broth."*

This is a real-world "flexible carnivore" protocol — beef-dominant, seafood as regular variety.

### Carnivore + Seafood Restaurant Strategy

> *"We've gone to her favorite restaurants, which are seafood restaurants, and I just have — I can have fish and all any seafood — I gladly feast on and still maintain my carnivore diet."*
> — Video: pBbUwQ6Jidw

**Practical note:** Seafood restaurants are one of the easiest places to maintain strict carnivore while eating out — grilled fish, shellfish, shrimp with butter are typically available at any seafood restaurant.

### Cooking Discovery Through Carnivore (Video: sxnYqjZXwrI)

> *"I never cooked before really. But ever since I started carnivore, I cook — and now I cook all the meat and I'm making crab cakes and I'm making oyster fritters. I'm like, 'Oh, wow.'"*

**n=1 observation:** Carnivore pushed someone to cook for the first time; seafood variety (oysters, crab) increased culinary engagement.

### Carnivore in Thailand: Fish-Heavy Adaptation (Video: pJMS6asEQx8)

> *"They provided carnivore food for me, which was in Thailand, as you can imagine, a lot of chicken, a lot of fish and and a bit of beef. But I trained 6, 7 hours a day."*

**Real-world adaptation:** In countries where beef is expensive or unavailable, fish becomes the primary carnivore protein. High training volume sustained on fish + chicken.

### Grizzly Bear Analogy (Video: jJD86UVQE9Y)

> *"There is a project in Canada where they following grizzly bears and watching them getting fed going into hibernation... this bear sitting in the river eating salmon all day long and I kind of said — okay, well if bear is a carnivore and it eats salmon..."*

Used as an ancestral/natural-world argument that apex carnivores eat fish — the grizzly bear as a proxy for what "true carnivore" in nature looks like.

### Shrimp When Protein Goals Are Hard to Hit (Video: hnYnaqOsw3U)

> *"I find it hard. I have to add things like shrimp sometimes... so sometimes I'll do things like that [to hit protein targets]."*

**Practical use case:** Shrimp as a lean, quick-cooking protein when appetite is low or hitting macros is difficult.

### Dr. Boz (Annette Bosworth) — Oily Fish Advocacy (zerocarb transcripts)

> *"She very much advocates for a carnivore diet. She very much advocates for using lots of oily fish and things like that to up [fat/omega-3 content]."*

Dr. Boz's approach: oily fish (sardines, salmon, mackerel) as a fat and omega-3 source to optimize ketosis on carnivore.

### Group Celebration: Steak + Shrimp (Video: Qi2adEZVkGk)

> *"When we were a year into carnivore, all my girlfriends went out to celebrate my year being carnivore and every single person did not drink and ordered steak and shrimp."*

Steak + shrimp = the de facto social "carnivore celebration meal."

### Community Advice for Beginners (Video: zFYoaKy7P3o)

> *"I see all the advice — lots of people saying: just eat meat and fat and fish and eggs and that's all you need — it is all you need. But then I see 'I failed, I'm not feeling well, I've got diarrhea...'"*

**Context:** Beginners are told meat + fat + fish + eggs is sufficient; adaptation issues are common in the first weeks regardless of protein source.

## 4. Metabolic Mind (`metabolicmind_transcripts.txt`)

Metabolic Mind focuses on psychiatric/cognitive applications of low-carb and carnivore diets. Seafood appears in clinical/institutional contexts.

### Clinical Setting: Memory Care Residents (metabolicmind transcripts)

A physician describes implementing carnivore for patients:

> *"I'm just like wow this really makes a difference. I thought this is going to be a little hard to convince my residents and their family that hey, you're just going to eat meat and fish and eggs and that's all we're going to do. So as I looked into more of the carnivore diet..."*

> *"You don't always have to buy grass-fed beef — we're seeing changes with our residents with grocery store beef. Same with fish. And you can use chicken as well."*

**Clinical n=1 observation:** Patients in a memory care setting responded positively to a meat + fish + eggs protocol using conventional (non-organic, non-wild-caught) grocery store fish. Accessibility was prioritized over premium sourcing.

### Policy / Nutrition Advocacy Framing

> *"You don't have to go carnivore, but animal source foods have a role. Meat, fish, eggs, and dairy are nutritious foods."*

Fish listed as a foundational animal food in the context of reversing plant-food-only dietary dogma.

## 6. Practical Takeaways

**Who should strongly consider seafood on carnivore:**
- Anyone eating predominantly **grain-finished beef** (omega-6 heavy → need omega-3 from fish)
- Anyone with **iodine concerns** (especially if not using iodized salt)
- Anyone with **iron/ferritin issues** (shellfish copper content aids retention)
- Anyone with **low testosterone** (oysters, crab for zinc)
- Anyone struggling with **appetite/volume** (shrimp and fish are lean, easy to eat)
- Anyone in a **country where beef is expensive or scarce**

**Best seafood choices for carnivore (from transcript mentions):**
1. **Smoked wild-caught salmon** — Baker's go-to for convenience
2. **Fresh salmon** — Baker + ZeroCarb community
3. **Sardines** — Chaffee guest sardine fasts; Dr. Boz for ketosis fat
4. **Shrimp** — Baker, ZeroCarb community for protein flexibility
5. **Oysters** — Zinc, copper; carnivore "superfood" for micronutrients
6. **Mackerel** — Omega-3, vitamin D (Baker mentions)
7. **Crab / lobster** — Zinc, testosterone support (Baker)

**Sourcing noted in transcripts:** Wild-caught preferred; Costco cited by Baker as accessible source. MetabolicMind clinical context: conventional grocery store fish works fine.
