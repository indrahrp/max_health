Editor's letter
Sasha Lin
Editor-in-chief

Cogitra was started in 2023 because we believed that the fastest way to understand the frontier of a science is, paradoxically, the slowest. The frontier is not the headline. It is the patient work that arrives after the headline cycle has moved on.

We publish across nine sections — Artificial Intelligence, Physical Health, Mental Health, Biology, Mindset, Genetics, Longevity, Physics, and Book Review. They are not separate magazines. They are connected by a common assumption: that the interesting questions are usually at the boundaries between fields, and that the only way to write across boundaries is slowly.

Thank you for reading.

Principles 04 commitments
01
Slow over fast
We will not file a piece to beat a news cycle. The interesting story is almost always the one that takes another week.
02
Specific over broad
The body of an essay is a list of specific things. We chase the specific, even when it costs us the headline.
03
Patient over clever
The trick is rarely the trick. We prefer the slow accumulation of evidence over the elegant single-paragraph case.
04
Edited, not generated
Every essay is written by a person, edited by another person, fact-checked by a third. We do not publish synthetic copy.
